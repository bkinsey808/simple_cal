Tu Bishvat, Rosh HaShanah La'Ilanot: a minor Jewish holiday, occurring on the 15th day of the Hebrew month of Shevat (in 2013, Tu Bishvat started from sunset on 25 January and finished at nightfall on 26 January). Tu Bishvat is the Israeli Arbor Day, and it is often referred to by that name in international media.[14] Ecological organizations in Israel and the diaspora have adopted the holiday to further environmental-awareness programs. On Israeli kibbutzim, Tu Bishvat is celebrated as an agricultural holiday.
