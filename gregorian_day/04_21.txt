Grounation Day: {Rastafarian} An important Rastafarian holy day, and second after Coronation Day (November 2). It is celebrated in honor of Haile Selassie's 1966 visit to Jamaica. Journalists were puzzled by Selassie's refusal to walk on the red carpet [hence "grounation", dialectal equivalent of "grounding" in the sense of "making contact with the soil"] on the way to his limousine.

Festival of Ridvan: {Bahai} (April 21-May 2): The annual Baha’i festival commemorates the 12 days (April 21-May 2, 1863) when Baha’u’llah, the founder of the Baha’i Faith, resided in a garden called Ridvan (Paradise) in Baghdad, Iraq. At this time He publicly proclaimed His mission as God’s messenger for this age. The first (April 21), ninth (April 29) and twelfth (May 2) days are celebrated as holy days when work is suspended.

San Jacinto Day: {Texas} San Jacinto Day is the celebration of the Battle of San Jacinto on April 21, 1836. It was the final battle of the Texas Revolution where Texas won its independence from Mexico. It is an official holiday according to the State of Texas

Parilia I: {Roman} Originally a country festival on which sheep were ritually purified against disease. Later also a commemoration of the birthday of the city of Rome. Each area in Rome had its own festivities, including bonfires and a large outdoor feast. In the second century AD, such was the popularity of games that they began to be held on this day. A special feature of these Ludi was the hunting of roes and hares in the Circus. Festival for smaller livestock
